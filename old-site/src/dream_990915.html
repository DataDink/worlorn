tenth tale
sharra goes home		 







		
Date: Wed, 15 Sep 1999 06:59:55 -0700
Subject: tenth tale
Maja held in one hand an apple, and in the other a small handwritten journal
from her library. She was seated on the lowest tread of a ladder that was
mounted on wheels, so it slid in a track along the wall of tall bookshelves.
Maja rolled slightly side to side, humming to herself and reading from the
book, occasionally taking a loud crispy bite from the apple. 
In the hall outside, Sharra stopped and quoted to herself, "'It's a tough
job, but somebody's got to do it.'" She knocked and entered so fast that
Maja had time only to look up from her page and swallow a last bite of apple
before Sharra stood before her.
"We must talk," said Sharra.
"You're wearing your going-away face."
"I am returning to Roke," said Sharra. 
Maja closed her book and placed it on the floor. "Why?"
"To report. Four turfholders have stolen the slaver from the Cave of the Mara.
My tenure as baby sitter is terminated." 
Maja stood up and began to pace the room. She bit from her apple and
chewed it thoughtfully. "Which Archetypes?"
"Preservation, Secrecy, Transformation, and Protection."
"Why?"
"The Star of Preservation expressed a desire to study the Thrint in
controlled circumstance."
"Can Wild Magic Dominate a Dominator?"
"The Thrint's unconsciousness was Preserved."
Maja whistled and turned to Sharra. "How did they know where it was?"
"I led them to it."
"The Decameron's tenth tale has ended? You go to claim Roke Mastery?"
Maja blinked and her gaze fell sharply on Sharra. "Wait. They joined
Dhruva's avatar here? Four Turfholder Avatars in my father's house?"
"Six. The Maker of Rules guided Marlee here to deploy them strategically,
and your narcoleptic Dark Childe guest, Muriel, awoke briefly to represent
The Star of Fire."
Before Sharra finished her sentence, Maja stopped pacing to lean her head
and empty hand against the bookshelf. She closed her eyes, holding the
pose for two breaths, and then pounded the wood with her free hand.
"Damn! Damn! Damn! A half dozen Turfholders in my Father's House?
Where was I when all this happened?"
Sharra shrugged. "It was a Secret Crossing-in-the-Mist. Your ignorance
was Preserved."
"Yours was not?" Maja accused.
Sharra smiled. "I'm special."
"Bitch."
"Tart."
"Wanton."
"Eggroll." The women smiled.
"How long will you be away?"
"I will be off the Troad calendar."
"This trip could take you through your full cycle." Maja said thoughtfully.
"Immortality."
Sharra shrugged. "It could kill me. Dead."
Maja piffled, and whispered, "Not bloody likley." She leaned her head against
the books again. "Everyone leaves me. Father, Mervin, Rune, then mother,
now you."
"You got Durvail back."
"Please stop using her name. She's going into hiding again. I gave her the
Flags Morgan left me, so she's out looking for peridot--"
Sharra became very still. "You stupid twit."
"What?"
"I told you to keep them safe. You have not resolved the Possession you
cast on Derrick, yet you give away Flags to someone who will use them
within the Radius of your Place of Power? Three overlapping radius?
How dumb can you be?"
"What?"
"Maja, this city already has two sets of inheritable Flags. Almitra's
son still controls a Radius here. You think for one moment that Dur--
mother would not use them to reclaim her tower here?"
"Maybe not. If the slaver's been taken away, mother can establish a
radius in secret somehere else. Besides, Simon's long gone, and my
inheritance was consumed by an illithid."
"Your mother came back. A chance exists that your inheritance exists."
It was Maja's turn to become very still. "What chance? You think the
gith children can aid me?"
"I think you should ask them, girl! Don't just abuse Derrick. You owe the
poor boy. You used him as rudely as your mother used Salt Peter. How do--"
"How dare you!"
"Am I wrong?"
"How dare you!"
"Am. I. Wrong?"
"What I did has nothing to do with that. Nothing!"
"Yes. You have not raped Derrick in Secret. No, you Summoned him from his
birthworld without permission or choice, forced him to participate in a
quest where he has nothing invested and nothing to gain, and when you
got in a tight spot, you used an untested Possession from Morgan la Faye's
private spellbook to exchange flesh against his will and thereby cost
him his life and Inspiration." The women glared at each other. Sharra
dropped into a deadly monotone. "I Destroy my enemies, Maj. It takes
seconds, leaving only phlogisten and ash. They do not writhe in agony
for the rest of their lives."
"How dare you?"
"I think you owe Derrick more than an apology. I think you need to study
your precious prophesy. Maja, you are not as quick as you think. If I
were you I would be finding ways to beg Derrick's forgiveness, so he
might someday consider restoring my precious inheritance." 
Maja Barusse's legs collapsed and she fell to the floor, cradling her head
in her arms, hands over her ears. Her apple rolled across the rugs. Sharra
walked to stand over Maja without a shred of compassion in her face. 
"You cannot run from this one. You do not have any coin from Derrick's
birthworld. I suggest that you get Morgan's Flags back from your mother
to use as an opening bid. Else you may be joining Tanri Silverharp when
she returns to your homeland, outworlder." Sharra turned away and walked
from the room, pausing in the doorway. "I hope things work out. I will
see you when I get back." 
Sharra closed the door, softly, with both hands. She looked up and down the
empty hall and put her left hand on a small table. Sharra leaned on the
table, pressing her eyelids with the fingertips and thumb of her other
hand.
